# Best-Designed iOS Apps

Apps worth studying for their design craft. Not just visually polished — these have well-thought-out interactions, clear information hierarchy, and genuine respect for iOS conventions.

---

## Camera & Photos

- **[Halide Mark III](https://halide.cam/)** (Lux Optics) — The gold standard for third-party iOS camera apps. Every control is deliberately placed, gesture-driven, and feels like it could have shipped with the OS. Study its bottom sheet, histogram, and exposure controls.
- **[Kino](https://www.lux.camera/kino/)** (Lux Optics) — Cinematic video from the same team as Halide. Beautiful UI, thoughtful onboarding. Same DNA as Halide.
- **[Darkroom](https://darkroom.co/)** — Photo and video editor with a UI that handles complexity without feeling complicated. Great use of contextual toolbars and bottom sheets.

---

## Productivity & Tasks

- **[Things 3](https://culturedcode.com/things/)** (Cultured Code) — Multiple Apple Design Award winner. Minimal, fast, and deeply considered. Study how it handles hierarchy without visual clutter. The quick entry panel is a masterclass.
- **[Fantastical](https://flexibits.com/fantastical/)** (Flexibits) — Apple Design Award winner. One of the most complex apps on iOS — multiple views, widgets, natural language input, integrations — and it never feels overwhelming.
- **[OmniFocus 4](https://www.omnigroup.com/omnifocus)** (Omni Group) — Deep task management that shows how to scale complexity for power users while remaining usable for everyone.

---

## Notes & Writing

- **[Bear](https://bear.app/)** (Shiny Frog) — Notes app with exceptional typography and a genuinely pleasant writing experience. The color-coded tag system and editor transitions are especially well done.
- **[iA Writer](https://ia.net/writer)** — Distraction-free writing with a design philosophy to match. Focused, deliberate, nothing unnecessary. Apple Design Award finalist.
- **[Craft](https://www.craft.do/)** — Document editor that makes structured long-form content feel native on iOS. Block-based editing with beautiful animations.

---

## Journaling

- **[Day One](https://dayoneapp.com/)** — Journaling done right. Clean, calm UI. The onboarding flow and streak UI are worth studying.

---

## Podcast & Reading

- **[Overcast](https://overcast.fm/)** (Marco Arment) — Built by one person. Every feature is considered and the playback UX is consistently better than apps with 10x the team behind them.
- **[Reeder](https://reederapp.com/)** — RSS reader famous for its gesture navigation. Swiping through articles in Reeder is the benchmark for gesture-based content browsing.
- **[NetNewsWire](https://netnewswire.com/)** — Open source RSS reader with a clean, straightforward iOS design. Great example of doing "just enough" well.

---

## Weather

- **[Carrot Weather](https://www.meetcarrot.com/weather/)** — Has a defined personality that carries through every screen, widget, and notification. Shows that apps can have character without sacrificing usability.
- **[Mercury Weather](https://mercuryweather.app/)** — Minimal, elegant weather UI. Great example of how to present complex data simply.

---

## Finance

- **[Copilot](https://copilot.money/)** — Personal finance app with an interface that makes dense financial data readable and even enjoyable. Charts, transactions, and budgets handled beautifully.

---

## Social & Communication

- **[Ivory](https://tapbots.com/ivory/)** (Tapbots) — Mastodon client by the team behind Tweetbot. Tapbots makes some of the most deliberately designed iOS apps — attention to animation, sound, and interaction is unmatched.
- **[Mona](https://mastodon.social/@MonaApp)** — Another excellent Mastodon client with a clean, fast UI and excellent use of native iOS components.

---

## Creative & Drawing

- **[Procreate](https://procreate.com/)** (Savage Interactive) — The benchmark for professional creative tools on iPad. Made touch feel like a legitimate creative medium. Study its tool palette, gesture system, and canvas controls.

---

## Utilities

- **[Widgetsmith](https://widgetsmith.app/)** — App entirely built around iOS Widgets. Great reference for widget design patterns across all sizes.
- **[Structured](https://structured.app/)** — Day planner with a timeline UI that's genuinely beautiful and functional. Great color use and iconography.

---

## Where to Learn from These Apps

Don't just download them — study them.

1. Notice what they **don't** include. Restraint is as important as features.
2. Pay attention to **transitions and animations**. The best ones feel inevitable.
3. Look at how they **handle empty states and onboarding**.
4. Watch how they use **native components** vs. building custom ones.
5. Check their **App Store screenshots** — great apps usually have great marketing too.
