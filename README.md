# iOS Design Resources

A hand-picked collection of tools, references, and inspiration for designing iOS apps. The good stuff only.

---

## Start Here

These are the absolute must-haves. Bookmark all of them before anything else.

- [Apple Human Interface Guidelines](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/) — The bible for iOS design. Covers layout, controls, patterns, and principles across every Apple platform. Re-read it every year.
- [Apple Design Resources](https://developer.apple.com/design/resources/) — Official Figma kits, app icon templates, device frames, and SF Symbols — straight from Apple.
- [SF Symbols](https://developer.apple.com/sf-symbols/) — 6,000+ symbols designed to work seamlessly with iOS. The app lets you browse, customize, and export at any weight or scale.
- [WWDC Design Sessions](https://developer.apple.com/videos/design/) — Apple designers explaining the thinking behind iOS design decisions. Free, searchable back to 2014.

## UI Kits & Templates

- [iOS 26 & iPadOS 26 for Figma](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1527721578857867021/ios-and-ipados-26) — Apple's official Figma kit with Liquid Glass components. Free.
- [iOS 18 & iPadOS 18 for Figma](https://www.figma.com/community/file/1247769024068708989/apple-ios-ui-kit) — Apple's official Figma kit for iOS 18 with full system component coverage. Free.
- [Apple on Figma Community](https://www.figma.com/@apple) — All official Apple design resources in one place.
- [iOS UI Kits Hub on Figma](https://www.figma.com/community/ui-kits/iphone) — Figma's curated collection of iPhone UI kits from the community. Free.
- [Setproduct iOS UI Kit](https://www.setproduct.com/templates/full-ios) — 3,300+ component variants with dark/light modes and auto-layout. One of the most complete paid kits. Paid.
- [UI8 iOS UI Kits](https://ui8.net/categories/ui-kits?filters=ios) — Marketplace of premium Figma and Sketch iOS kits from independent designers. Paid.

## Inspiration & Pattern References

Real apps. Real flows. Real design decisions.

- [Mobbin](https://mobbin.com/) — 400,000+ iOS screenshots from the world's best apps, searchable by screen type, component, and flow. The go-to for pattern research. Free & paid.
- [Refero](https://refero.design/apps) — AI-powered search across real product screens. Great when you know what you're looking for.
- [ScreensDesign](https://screensdesign.com/) — How successful apps design their screens, onboarding, and paywalls. Beautifully curated.
- [Pttrns](https://pttrns.com/) — One of the oldest and most trusted mobile UI pattern galleries. Organized by interaction type. Free & paid.
- [Spotted in Prod](https://www.spottedinprod.com/) — Unusual and interesting UI details spotted in real, live apps.
- [UI Sources](https://www.uisources.com/) — Recorded real user flows from popular apps — actual animations and swipes, not just static screenshots. Free & paid.
- [Page Flows](https://pageflows.com/) — Recorded user flow videos from onboarding, checkout, and key screens in dozens of popular apps. Paid.
- [Screenlane](https://screenlane.com/) — Curated mobile design inspiration filterable by screen type and category.
- [Dribbble](https://dribbble.com/) — Not always realistic, but great for visual direction and early concept exploration.

## Paywall & Monetization

Essential when designing for subscriptions.

- [PaywallPro](https://www.paywallpro.app/) — 26,000+ iOS paywall screenshots with pricing models, copy strategies, and conversion signals from top apps.
- [Paywall Screens](https://www.paywallscreens.com/) — Curated paywall designs from real apps.
- [Mobbin Paywall Screens](https://mobbin.com/explore/mobile/screens/subscription-paywall) — Paywall and subscription screens filtered directly in Mobbin.

## Icons

- [SF Symbols](https://developer.apple.com/sf-symbols/) — Start here for every icon need. 6,000+ symbols with 9 weights, animation support, and multicolor variants.
- [Icon Composer](https://developer.apple.com/icon-composer/) — Apple's official tool for creating layered app icons with dark and tinted variants.
- [Phosphor Icons](https://phosphoricons.com/) — 9,000+ icons in 6 styles. Figma-friendly and perfect for iOS mockups where SF Symbols don't fit. Free.
- [Ionicons](https://ionic.io/ionicons) — Premium icon set with dedicated iOS-style variants built in. Open source.
- [Streamline Icons](https://www.streamlinehq.com/) — Massive library across many styles with a generous free tier. Free & paid.

## Prototyping

For when static mocks aren't enough.

- [ProtoPie](https://www.protopie.io/) — The gold standard for advanced iOS interactions and micro-animations. Sensor-aware (gyroscope, camera, touch). Used by Spotify, Amazon, Microsoft. Paid.
- [Origami Studio](https://origami.design/) — Meta's free, powerful prototyping tool built for complex iOS interactions. Steep learning curve, deep results. Free.
- [Principle](https://principleformac.com/) — Fast, timeline-based animations with a Principle Mirror app for live iPhone preview. Mac-only. $129 one-time.
- [Framer](https://www.framer.com/) — Design + code prototyping with React component support. Great for hi-fi interactive demos. Free & paid.

## Design Tools

- [Figma](https://www.figma.com/) — The primary tool for most iOS designers. Collaborative, cross-platform, with a huge iOS component ecosystem.
- [Sketch](https://www.sketch.com/) — Mac-native with a deep iOS plugin ecosystem. Still the preference for many native iOS designers. Paid.

---

## Mockups & Presentation

Show your work in context.

- [Rotato](https://rotato.app/) — The best tool for 3D animated iPhone mockups and App Store marketing videos. Outstanding output quality. Paid (~$49/yr).
- [MockUPhone](https://mockuphone.com/) — Free browser-based device frame wrapper. Fast, no account required.
- [Device Frames](https://deviceframes.com/) — 3D device mockup generator for iPhone and iPad, browser-based.
- [AppScreens](https://appscreens.com/) — Create professional App Store screenshots with device frames and copy overlays. Free & paid.
- [Zeplin](https://zeplin.io/) — Design handoff that generates iOS measurements, UIColor values, and asset exports from Figma/Sketch files. Paid (free tier available).

## Typography & Color

- [Apple System Colors (HIG)](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/color) — Official iOS system colors with light/dark mode values, semantic roles, and contrast guidance.
- [Apple Fonts — SF Pro & New York](https://developer.apple.com/fonts/) — Download San Francisco and New York for use in Figma and Sketch. Free.
- [iOS Font Size Guidelines](https://www.learnui.design/blog/ios-font-size-guidelines.html) — Practical guide to Dynamic Type, iOS type scales, and recommended text style sizes.
- [Coolors Contrast Checker](https://coolors.co/contrast-checker) — Fast WCAG contrast checker for any color pair. Essential.
- [Contrasts (macOS)](https://contrasts.app/) — Native macOS app for checking contrast ratios directly from any on-screen source. Paid.

---

## Accessibility

- [Apple Accessibility (HIG)](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/accessibility) — The official guide to accessible iOS design: VoiceOver, Dynamic Type, contrast, motion, and more.
- [Apple Accessibility Developer Hub](https://developer.apple.com/accessibility/) — APIs, WWDC sessions, and resources for building accessible apps.
- [Orange Digital Accessibility iOS Guidelines](https://a11y-guidelines.orange.com/en/mobile/ios/design/) — The most thorough iOS accessibility checklist available — maps every WCAG criterion to practical mobile design decisions.
- [Coolors Contrast Checker](https://coolors.co/contrast-checker) — Quick AA/AAA ratio check when picking text/background pairs.

## Learning

Articles, courses, and videos worth your time.

- [Design+Code](https://designcode.io/ios-design) — Comprehensive tutorials for designing and building iOS apps in Figma + SwiftUI. Updated for iOS 18 and SwiftUI 6. Paid.
- [Learn UI Design: iOS 26 Guidelines](https://www.learnui.design/blog/ios-design-guidelines-templates.html) — Illustrated breakdown of iOS design patterns with free templates. Excellent quick reference.
- [Big Mountain Studio SwiftUI Books](https://www.bigmountainstudio.com/) — Visual reference books for SwiftUI — great if you design close to the code.
- [Hacking with Swift](https://www.hackingwithswift.com/) — World's largest free Swift learning site. Valuable for understanding what's natively buildable in iOS. Free.
- [100 Days of SwiftUI](https://www.hackingwithswift.com/100/swiftui) — The best free structured course on SwiftUI from scratch.
- [Kavsoft on YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/@Kavsoft) — SwiftUI animation and UI tutorials. Beautiful component builds worth watching even if you don't code. Free.
- [iOS App Design on Udemy](https://www.udemy.com/course/app-design-uiux-plus-ios-development/) — Affordable course covering iOS UI/UX design and development together.
